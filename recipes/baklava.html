Baklava

Turkish baklava is a sweet and indulgent pastry made of thin layers of flaky dough, filled with chopped nuts and sweetened with syrup.

Ingredients:

  • Phyllo dough
  • Butter
  • Chopped nuts (pistachios, walnuts, or almonds)
  • Sugar
  • Water
  • Lemon juice
  • Rose or orange blossom water (optional, for flavoring the syrup)

Steps on the Recipe:

  1. Prepare the Nut Filling:

    Combine finely chopped pistachios or walnuts with half a cup of sugar. Mix well and set aside.

  2. Prepare the Syrup:

    In a saucepan, combine the remaining half cup of sugar, water, and lemon juice. Cook until sugar dissolves and the syrup slightly thickens. Remove from heat and add rose or orange blossom water if using. Let it cool.

  3. Assemble the Baklava:

    Preheat the oven to 175 Celsius. Brush a baking dish with melted butter. Layer 8-10 sheets of phyllo dough, brushing each layer with butter. Add a layer of the nut mixture. Repeat until you run out of nuts, finishing with a layer of phyllo dough on top, generously brushed with butter.

  4. Cut and Bake:

    Cut the baklava into diamond or square shapes using a sharp knife. Bake for 30-35 minutes until golden brown and crispy.

  5. Pour the Syrup:

    Pour the cooled syrup evenly over the hot baklava, allowing it to soak and infuse the layers. Let it cool completely.

  6. Serve and Enjoy:

    Once cooled and the syrup is absorbed, your Turkish baklava is ready to be served. Enjoy!
