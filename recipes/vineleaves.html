Vine Leaves Rolls

Vine Leaves Rolls, also known as Dolma or Sarma, are a classic Middle Eastern and Mediterranean dish featuring vine leaves filled with a flavorful mixture of rice, herbs, and spices. They are rolled, cooked, and served as a delicious appetizer or main course.

Ingredients:

  • Vine leaves
  • Long-grain rice
  • Onions
  • Tomatoes
  • Herbs (such as parsley and dill)
  • Spices (such as mint, paprika, and cinnamon)

Steps on the Recipe:

  1. Prepare the Filling:

    Mix cooked long-grain rice with finely chopped onions, tomatoes, herbs (such as parsley and dill), and a blend of spices like mint, paprika, and cinnamon to create a flavorful filling.

  2. Prepare the Vine Leaves:

    If using preserved vine leaves, gently rinse them to remove excess salt. If using fresh vine leaves, blanch them in boiling water for a few minutes, then drain and pat dry.

  3. Fill and Roll:

    Place a small amount of the filling in the center of each vine leaf. Fold the sides and roll tightly to form a compact roll.

  4. Cook the Rolls:

    Arrange the rolled vine leaves in a pot, seam side down, creating layers. Add water, lemon juice, and olive oil. Cover and simmer over low heat until the vine leaves are tender and the filling is cooked through.

  5. Serve and Enjoy:

    Once cooked, let the vine leaves rolls cool slightly before serving. Garnish with fresh herbs and serve with yogurt or a squeeze of lemon. Enjoy your flavorful homemade vine leaves rolls!
